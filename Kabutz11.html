World Music Day Special:
Voices Behind the Industry Speak Out

Music is a universal language that keeps evolving from tradition to digital, from local stages to global audiences.

History of music August Alban, Producer Jeniffer Nora, Producer Aurisya Koswara Kwando, Soloist Celeste Vivienne, Soloist Our Dream, Boy Group

Music is one of the art forms closest to human life. Through rhythm, melody, and lyrics, music becomes a medium for expressing emotions, delivering messages, and building connections between people beyond language and cultural barriers. From traditional melodies to modern digital productions, music continues to evolve alongside society and remains an important part of everyday life.

Every year on June 21, people around the world celebrate World Music Day, also known as Fete de la Musique, which was first introduced in France in 1982. The celebration was created as a way to appreciate music and everyone involved in the creative industry behind it. World Music Day also encourages people to enjoy music freely while supporting musicians and artists who continue to inspire audiences through their work.

History of music

The history of music dates back thousands of years, long before written language. Prehistoric people used natural sounds, hand claps, and simple stone or wood instruments to create rhythm. Music evolved through ancient civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and China, where instruments like harps and flutes symbolized cultural growth. The Middle Ages and Renaissance brought musical notation and orchestral performances, leading to classical masters like Mozart and Beethoven.

In ancient civilizations such as Egypt, Greece, and China, music began to develop its own systems and theories. The ancient Greeks considered music an essential part of education and social life. Instruments such as harps, flutes, and lyres became symbols of cultural development at the time. Music was not only seen as entertainment, but also believed to influence human emotions and character.

Entering the Middle Ages and the Renaissance period in Europe, music developed rapidly with the introduction of musical notation and orchestral performances. Composers began creating works that were more structured and artistic. During this period, classical music gained widespread recognition and became the foundation for the development of modern music in the future. Legendary composers such as Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart and Ludwig van Beethoven later became iconic figures in world music history.

Technological advancements in the 20th century brought major changes to the music industry. The rise of radio, vinyl records, cassette tapes, and eventually digital platforms made music more accessible to global audiences. Various genres such as jazz, rock, pop, hip hop, and electronic music emerged and developed with their own unique characteristics. Music eventually became not only a form of entertainment, but also a massive industry driven by creativity, technology, and business.

In today's modern era, music continues to transform alongside the growth of social media and digital streaming platforms. Artists are now able to share their work with worldwide audiences more quickly and easily than ever before. Cross-cultural and international collaborations have also become increasingly common, creating new colors and diversity within the global music industry.

To celebrate World Music Day, many producers, soloists, and idol groups from around the world have shared their perspectives on what music means to them and how they create their work behind the scenes. Through interviews and discussions, they explain how music becomes a way to express stories, emotions, and personal experiences to listeners. They also share insights into the creative and production process of making music in today's fast-moving and highly competitive digital industry.

August Alban, Producer

In celebration of World Music Day, various figures from the music industry have begun sharing their perspectives on creativity, artistic identity, and the challenges of surviving in today's rapidly evolving digital era. One of them is August Alban, a producer known for his reflective approach to music production and creative process.

In an interview session with Kaboom, August Alban spoke about the growing pressure within today's music industry, where artists and producers are often expected to continuously release new projects in a short amount of time. According to him, maintaining quality and preserving personal identity in music has become one of the biggest challenges producers face today.

"People think being a producer means you're always 'on,' always cranking out beats on demand. And sure, sometimes it is like that. But the songs people actually remember, the ones that hold up years later, those almost never came from a session where I felt rushed," he explained during the interview.

August also shared that, for him, a producer's identity is not defined solely by technical elements such as drums or chord progressions, but by the subtle creative decisions made throughout the production process. He emphasized that authenticity often comes from details that listeners may not immediately notice, yet emotionally feel within the music itself.

"The way I let a vocal breathe before the beat drops back in. The texture I choose over a cleaner option because it feels more honest. Those choices take time, and time is the one thing the industry doesn't want to give you," he said.

He further revealed that whenever he feels disconnected from his artistic identity, he chooses to step away from external pressure and return to the simplest form of music-making. According to him, reconnecting with the original reason behind creating music is what allows him to continue producing work that feels genuine and emotionally real.

Beyond discussing production pressure, August Alban also reflected on how technology and social media have transformed the music industry in the digital age. While he acknowledged that modern accessibility has opened opportunities for independent creators, he also admitted that the current system has made the creative process more emotionally demanding for producers.

"I didn't go to some elite production school with connections. I was a kid with a cracked laptop and free software, learning by breaking things and figuring out how to fix them. Without that accessibility, there's no version of me that ends up where I am," he shared.

However, he believes the meaning of "finishing" a song has drastically changed compared to previous generations. Producers are now expected not only to create music, but also to think about promotion strategies, social media presentation, online framing, and digital rollout plans even before audiences fully experience the song itself.

"As a producer, that adds this whole second job that has nothing to do with sound, but it still affects how you make the sound, because you're aware of it the entire time," August explained.

Despite the growing complexity of the industry, he believes creativity itself still begins from genuine emotion rather than algorithms or online trends. For him, the emotional connection behind a song will always exist before numbers, platforms, or digital marketing strategies enter the conversation.

"So creatively, I think the tools opened doors that should've always been open. But emotionally, producers now carry this extra layer of noise that didn't used to be there," he concluded.

Jeniffer Nora, Producer

In another interview session with Kaboom, Jeniffer Nora shared her perspective on balancing artistic idealism with the demands of today's music industry trends. According to her, every production process should begin with personal instinct and genuine artistic vision before considering anything beyond the music itself.

"As a producer, I always start with instinct and artistic vision. For me, every song needs a genuine emotional foundation before anything else," Jeniffer Nora explained.

She stated that focusing too heavily on trends or what is currently popular can often make the final result feel less authentic and emotionally disconnected from the story the artist wants to tell. However, Jeniffer also acknowledged that the music industry and listener behavior continue to evolve rapidly in today's digital era.

According to her, streaming platforms, social media, and modern production styles have significantly changed the way audiences discover and experience music. Because of that, she believes it is still important for producers to stay aware of these developments in order to present songs in a way that feels current and engaging for listeners today.

"However, I see trends as references rather than directions. They can inspire certain production choices, whether it's a mixing approach, sound design, or arrangement technique, but they never define the heart of the song," she said.

Jeniffer added that the role of a producer is not only to create music that sounds modern, but also to preserve artistic honesty within the work itself. For her, the ultimate goal is to create songs that remain true to their identity while still being able to connect with modern audiences.

Beyond discussing industry trends, Jeniffer Nora also talked about creative blocks, a challenge that many artists and producers experience throughout their careers. According to her, creative blocks are a natural part of the creative journey and not something that should always be resisted.

"I've come to accept that creative blocks are a natural part of the creative journey. Instead of fighting them, I've learned that sometimes the best thing I can do is step away for a moment and give myself some space," she shared.

Whenever she feels stuck or loses motivation in the studio, Jeniffer usually chooses to temporarily step away from the project she is working on and spend time doing completely different activities outside the production environment. She explained that she often regains inspiration by spending time outdoors, playing instruments without pressure, writing random thoughts, or simply taking a walk to clear her mind.

In addition, she enjoys listening to music outside of the genres she normally works in. For her, inspiration can come from unexpected details, whether it is a sound texture, rhythm, or a unique production approach found in another song.

"Sometimes a small detail in a song can spark an entirely new idea. As a producer, I'm constantly inspired by different sounds, cultures, and creative approaches," Jeniffer explained.

At the end of the interview, Jeniffer Nora emphasized that creativity cannot always be forced. According to her, one of the most important parts of the creative process is reconnecting with the original reason why someone started making music in the first place.

"Music has always been a space where I can be honest with myself and express emotions that are difficult to put into words. When I return to that mindset, the pressure to create usually fades, and inspiration tends to come back naturally," she concluded.

Aurisya Koswara Kwando, Soloist

Continuing the World Music Day special interview series with Kaboom, Aurisya Koswara Kwando shared her thoughts on artistic identity, creative storytelling, and the emotional responsibility of performing as a soloist in today's music industry. This interview took place at Aurisya Private Studio, Central Jakarta.

During the interview at Aurisya Private Studio, Aurisya explained that her involvement in every song she creates goes far beyond simply recording music. According to her, songwriting, visual direction, storytelling, and performance concepts are all deeply connected and play an important role in shaping the emotional experience behind her work.

"My role in every song I write is obviously very significant, and it influences every aspect involved in the process. It starts with writing lyrics. I always try to pour themes of love and obsession into every song I create," she shared.

Aurisya revealed that she enjoys romanticizing emotions surrounding love and affection, often expressing the perspective of a woman deeply in love and longing to be loved wholeheartedly. For her, lyrics become the foundation that allows emotions to be delivered honestly and completely.

She also emphasized that visual elements such as artwork concepts, color tones, styling, makeup, expressions, and music video narratives must remain interconnected with the emotions carried by the song itself. In some cases, she personally prepares visual concepts before presenting them to the production team to ensure that every detail reflects the story behind the lyrics.

"I want every visual element to become an extension of the lyrics I write, but this time portrayed visually and more realistically. Because music is not only meant to be heard, but also seen and deeply felt," Aurisya explained.

According to her, contributing to every stage of production, from lyric writing to music video shoots, is important in building a stronger emotional connection between the audience and the music itself. She believes that when a song carries sweet and affectionate emotions, the visual representation should feel equally alive so listeners can fully immerse themselves in the same feeling.

As a soloist known for her playful, cute, and emotionally expressive public image, Aurisya stated that maintaining consistency in creative direction is also essential in preserving the identity she has built since the beginning of her career.

"I want my listeners to feel the same excitement and butterflies. When they think about something full of love, I want them to remember me as its icon," she said.

She also mentioned her personal slogan, "chanting spells to pull you into my arms," describing it as a representation of how she hopes her songs can become emotional spells delivered through music that listeners can experience in a deeply personal way.

"Being a soloist and musician is not only about singing songs. It's also about building a character and a world that listeners can emotionally experience," Aurisya added.

Beyond discussing creativity and artistic identity, Aurisya Koswara Kwando also spoke openly about the emotional pressure that often comes with performing alone on stage. According to her, despite occasionally performing alongside dancers, the attention of the audience ultimately remains focused on the solo performer.

"The pressure is definitely always there, especially as a soloist who has to stand alone on stage. Even when dancers are performing with me, people's eyes will still naturally focus on me," she explained.

To prepare mentally before performances, Aurisya shared that she tries to transform fear into trust rather than allowing pressure to overwhelm her. Instead of focusing on possible mistakes, she reminds herself why she chose to stand on stage and share her stories with others in the first place.

"Not everyone has the courage and opportunity to stand on stage and be heard. The reason I'm still here today is alsoembedbelieving in me," she said.

Emotionally, Aurisya admitted that managing nervousness remains one of the biggest challenges she faces before stepping onto the stage. However, instead of rejecting those emotions, she chooses to embrace them as part of the performance itself.

"Nervousness is natural. That means I truly enjoy and embrace every mixed feeling before stepping onto the stage," she shared.

According to Aurisya, emotions can easily be reflected through facial expressions and stage presence, which is why she tries to channel those feelings into energy that helps create a more transparent and emotionally connected performance for the audience.

"If I can feel connected to my emotions and the song I'm performing, the connection with the audience usually forms naturally," she explained.

At the end of the interview, Aurisya emphasized that the most memorable performances are not always the most technically perfect ones, but the performances capable of making audiences feel emotionally understood and connected.

"If someone leaves after my performance feeling accompanied, understood, or even reminded of someone they love because of the song I sang, then I feel like I've succeeded in fulfilling my role as a musician," she concluded.

Celeste Vivienne, Soloist

Celeste Vivienne shared her perspective on the relationship between artists and social media, as well as the growing challenges faced by solo performers in today's music industry. The interview session took place at Lunaria Studio Record alongside Kaboom.

During the interview at Lunaria Studio Record, Celeste Vivienne explained that social media has created a much closer connection between artists and audiences compared to previous generations. According to her, this development can feel deeply inspiring, while also bringing emotional pressure that many artists quietly experience behind the scenes.

"Social media is a beautiful paradox for me. On one hand, it's incredibly inspiring because I get to see how my music lives in people's everyday moments, how it becomes part of their stories, their healing, and their joy," Celeste shared.

She believes that this level of closeness makes music feel more human, immediate, and meaningful than ever before. What older generations of artists could once only imagine can now be experienced directly through digital interaction and online communities.

However, behind that connection, Celeste also acknowledged the emotional weight that comes with living under constant public visibility. According to her, social media creates an unspoken pressure for artists to always remain present, evolving, and worthy of attention in the eyes of the public.

"When your life is constantly visible, there's a quiet pressure to always be 'present,' always be evolving, and always be worthy of attention," she explained.

Celeste stated that this environment can slowly blur the line between an artist's identity and their personal life as an individual. Because of that, she believes it is important to protect a part of herself from public exposure and preserve a sense of privacy.

"I've learned that protecting a part of myself—something private and untouched—is essential. Because at the end of the day, the music needs honesty, and honesty needs space," she said.

Beyond discussing social media, Celeste Vivienne also reflected on the biggest challenges soloists face in today's music industry compared to previous generations. According to her, modern solo artists are now expected to carry multiple roles at the same time.

"You're not just an artist anymore. You also have to become a brand, a storyteller, a content creator, and a public figure. And all of that exists in real time under constant observation," she stated.

Celeste believes that artists from previous generations still had more space to grow quietly between releases, while today relevance moves at an extremely fast pace and trends can shift overnight.

For her, the biggest challenge is not simply following trends, but remaining grounded in one's identity while everything around them continues to change rapidly.

"That's the real challenge—staying grounded in who you are while everything around you is constantly changing," Celeste explained.

At the end of the interview, Celeste Vivienne emphasized that success should not be measured by how well an artist keeps up with every trend. Instead, she believes the most meaningful works are the ones that remain honest and timeless even years later.

"For me, success isn't about keeping up with every trend. It's about creating something timeless, something that still feels true years from now. Because in the end, authenticity is the only thing that doesn't go out of style," she concluded.

Our Dream, Boy Group

Leonardo Nicholas Wang, also known as Leo from Our Dream, shared his thoughts on teamwork, group chemistry, and the challenge of maintaining a strong musical identity in today's rapidly evolving music industry. The interview session took place at Kadio Space, The Kachoo Office Building, South Jakarta alongside Kaboom.

According to Leo, synchronization, chemistry, and trust between members are essential elements in delivering a powerful group performance on stage. He explained that behind every performance, there is continuous effort to maintain emotional closeness and healthy communication within the team.

"You're absolutely right. A group performance really requires strong synchronization, chemistry, and trust between members in order to give the best performance possible. We don't want to disappoint the fans who come to support us, right?" Leo said with a laugh.

To maintain strong relationships behind the scenes, Leo shared that the members usually build connection through simple moments together, such as eating together or spending time in the practice room during breaks. He also mentioned that the group's leader and management team play an important role in helping the members build chemistry naturally.

"Usually, we start with simple things first. Eating together or playing around during break time in the practice room helps us build chemistry naturally," he explained.

Leo also revealed that he personally feels close with all the members of Our Dream because he enjoys approaching people first and sharing hobbies or casual conversations with them.

"I'm not really the type of person who's shy about approaching people, so I usually approach the other members first and talk about things we both like," he said.

Despite the strong bond within the group, Leo admitted that differences in opinion and pressure are unavoidable, especially while preparing major projects such as comebacks. He shared that disagreements previously happened while discussing song selection and musical direction for their recent Korean comeback.

"Some members had pretty significant differences in opinion regarding the songs and the musical direction for our Korean comeback," Leo revealed.

However, rather than allowing conflicts to grow into larger problems, the members chose to solve them through discussion and collective decision-making. According to him, openness and communication are important in maintaining trust within the team.

"We usually solve things through discussion and voting so everything feels fair. If someone has objections, we encourage them to speak up at that moment," he explained.

Leo also mentioned that the members often joke around during practice sessions to keep the atmosphere from becoming too tense or emotionally exhausting.

Beyond discussing group chemistry, Leonardo Nicholas Wang also reflected on how quickly music trends continue to change in today's industry. According to him, keeping the group's music fresh while preserving its unique identity has become one of the biggest challenges for modern idol groups.

"It's true that music trends are constantly changing," he stated.

For Our Dream, continuing to evolve musically means releasing songs that feel fresh, relatable, and encouraging for listeners without losing the group's signature emotional color.

"The concept of teenagers and dreams has always been the main thread throughout Our Dream's journey because we want our music to accompany people through every stage of growth," Leo explained.

He hopes that Our Dream's music can support listeners while chasing dreams, facing challenges, or celebrating small achievements in life. Because of that, although their music continues evolving alongside modern trends, the themes of togetherness, hope, and courage to keep dreaming will always remain the heart of the group's identity.

"Our Dream means 'our dreams,' so we want to continue protecting our dreams and everyone else's dreams as well, so they can continue growing and eventually become reality while bringing positive things to the people around us," he concluded.

World Music Day reminds us that music is a shared heritage of humanity. From traditional melodies to digital experiments, every note carries stories and hopes. Let us continue to support musicians, appreciate their work, and let music be a bridge that unites differences. Because in the end, music is not just about sound, but about the feeling that touches the heart of every listener.

Spesial Hari Musik Sedunia:
Suara dari Balik Industri Musik dan Perkembangannya

Musik adalah bahasa universal yang terus berevolusi dari tradisi hingga digital, dari panggung lokal hingga global.

Sejarah tentang musik August Alban, Produser Jeniffer Nora, Produser Aurisya Koswara Kwando, Soloist Celeste Vivienne, Soloist Our Dream, Boy Group

Musik merupakan salah satu bentuk seni yang paling dekat dengan kehidupan manusia.Melalui irama, melodi, dan lirik, musik mampu menjadi media untuk mengekspresikan emosi, menyampaikan pesan, hingga membangun hubungan antarmanusia tanpa batas bahasa maupun budaya. Dari musik tradisional hingga karya modern berbasis teknologi digital, musik terus berkembang mengikuti zaman dan menjadi bagian penting dalam kehidupan sehari-hari.

Setiap tanggal 21 Juni, dunia memperingati Hari Musik Sedunia atau World Music Day yang pertama kali dipopulerkan di Prancis pada tahun 1982 dengan nama Fete de la Musique. Peringatan ini hadir sebagai bentuk apresiasi terhadap musik dan seluruh pelaku industri kreatif di dalamnya. Hari Musik Sedunia juga menjadi momentum untuk mengajak masyarakat menikmati musik secara bebas, sekaligus mendukung para musisi agar terus berkarya dan menyebarkan inspirasi melalui lagu-lagu mereka.

Sejarah tentang musik

Sejarah musik telah dimulai sejak ribuan tahun lalu, jauh sebelum manusia mengenal tulisan. Pada masa prasejarah, manusia menggunakan suara alam, tepukan tangan, serta alat sederhana dari batu dan kayu untuk menciptakan ritme. Musik pada masa itu umumnya digunakan dalam ritual adat, komunikasi, maupun upacara keagamaan. Seiring berkembangnya peradaban, alat musik mulai dibuat lebih kompleks dan beragam.

Di era peradaban kuno seperti Mesir, Yunani, dan Tiongkok, musik mulai memiliki aturan serta teori tersendiri. Bangsa Yunani bahkan menganggap musik sebagai bagian penting dalam pendidikan dan kehidupan sosial. Instrumen seperti harpa, seruling, dan kecapi menjadi simbol perkembangan budaya pada masa tersebut. Musik tidak hanya berfungsi sebagai hiburan, tetapi juga dipercaya mampu memengaruhi emosi dan karakter manusia.

Memasuki Abad Pertengahan hingga era Renaisans di Eropa, musik berkembang pesat melalui munculnya notasi musik dan pertunjukan orkestra. Komposer mulai menciptakan karya-karya yang lebih terstruktur dan artistik. Pada periode ini, musik klasik mulai dikenal luas dan menjadi fondasi bagi perkembangan musik modern di masa depan. Nama-nama besar seperti Mozart dan Beethoven kemudian menjadi simbol penting dalam sejarah musik dunia.

Perkembangan teknologi pada abad ke-20 membawa perubahan besar dalam industri musik. Kehadiran radio, piringan hitam, kaset, hingga platform digital membuat musik semakin mudah diakses oleh masyarakat global. Berbagai genre seperti jazz, rock, pop, hip hop, hingga elektronik lahir dan berkembang dengan karakteristiknya masing-masing. Musik kini tidak hanya menjadi hiburan, tetapi juga industri besar yang melibatkan kreativitas, teknologi, dan bisnis.

Di era modern saat ini, musik terus mengalami transformasi seiring berkembangnya media sosial dan platform streaming digital. Musisi dapat membagikan karya mereka secara lebih luas dan cepat kepada pendengar di seluruh dunia. Kolaborasi lintas negara dan budaya juga semakin sering terjadi, menciptakan warna baru dalam industri musik global yang semakin dinamis dan beragam.

Dalam rangka memperingati Hari Musik Sedunia, berbagai produser musik, solois, hingga idol grup dari berbagai negara turut membagikan pandangan mereka mengenai arti musik dalam kehidupan dan proses kreatif di balik karya-karya mereka. Melalui wawancara dan diskusi, mereka menceritakan bagaimana musik menjadi media untuk menyampaikan cerita, emosi, serta pengalaman pribadi kepada para pendengar. Tidak hanya itu, mereka juga membagikan proses produksi lagu di tengah perkembangan industri musik modern yang semakin kompetitif dan dipengaruhi oleh teknologi digital.

August Alban, Produser

Dalam rangka memperingati Hari Musik Sedunia, berbagai figur di industri musik mulai membagikan pandangan mereka mengenai kreativitas, identitas artistik, serta tantangan bertahan di era digital yang terus berubah dengan cepat. Salah satunya adalah August Alban, seorang produser yang dikenal dengan pendekatan reflektifnya dalam produksi musik dan proses kreatif.

Dalam sesi wawancara bersama Kaboom, August Alban berbicara tentang tekanan yang semakin besar di industri musik saat ini, di mana artis dan produser sering diharapkan untuk terus merilis proyek baru dalam waktu singkat. Menurutnya, menjaga kualitas dan mempertahankan identitas pribadi dalam musik telah menjadi salah satu tantangan terbesar yang dihadapi produser saat ini.

"Orang berpikir menjadi produser berarti kamu harus selalu 'on', selalu membuat irama sesuai permintaan. Dan memang, terkadang seperti itu. Tapi lagu yang benar-benar diingat orang, yang bertahan bertahun-tahun kemudian, hampir tidak pernah datang dari sesi di mana saya merasa terburu-buru," jelasnya dalam wawancara.

August juga berbagi bahwa, baginya, identitas produser tidak hanya ditentukan oleh elemen teknis seperti drum atau progresi akor, tetapi oleh keputusan kreatif halus yang dibuat selama proses produksi. Ia menekankan bahwa keaslian sering datang dari detail yang mungkin tidak langsung diperhatikan pendengar, namun secara emosional terasa dalam musik itu sendiri.

"Cara saya membiarkan vokal bernafas sebelum irama masuk kembali. Tekstur yang saya pilih daripada opsi yang lebih bersih karena terasa lebih jujur. Pilihan-pilihan itu membutuhkan waktu, dan waktu adalah satu hal yang tidak ingin diberikan industri kepada Anda," ujarnya.

Dia mengungkapkan bahwa setiap kali merasa terputus dari identitas artistiknya, ia memilih untuk menjauh dari tekanan eksternal dan kembali ke bentuk pembuatan musik yang paling sederhana. Menurutnya, terhubung kembali dengan alasan awal di balik penciptaan musik adalah apa yang memungkinkannya terus menghasilkan karya yang terasa otentik dan nyata secara emosional.

Di akhir wawancara, August menekankan bahwa meskipun teknologi telah membuka banyak pintu, secara emosional para produser sekarang membawa beban tambahan yang tidak ada sebelumnya.

Jeniffer Nora, Produser

Dalam sesi wawancara lainnya bersama Kaboom, Jeniffer Nora membagikan pandangannya tentang menyeimbangkan idealisme artistik dengan tuntutan tren industri musik saat ini. Menurutnya, setiap proses produksi harus dimulai dengan naluri pribadi dan visi artistik yang tulus sebelum mempertimbangkan hal lain di luar musik itu sendiri.

"Sebagai produser, saya selalu memulai dengan naluri dan visi artistik. Bagi saya, setiap lagu membutuhkan fondasi emosional yang tulus sebelum hal lainnya," jelas Jeniffer Nora.

Ia menyatakan bahwa terlalu fokus pada tren atau apa yang sedang populer sering kali membuat hasil akhir terasa kurang otentik dan terputus secara emosional dari cerita yang ingin disampaikan artis. Namun, Jeniffer juga mengakui bahwa industri musik dan perilaku pendengar terus berkembang pesat di era digital saat ini.

Menurutnya, platform streaming, media sosial, dan gaya produksi modern telah secara signifikan mengubah cara audiens menemukan dan mengalami musik. Karena itu, ia percaya penting bagi produser untuk tetap sadar akan perkembangan ini agar dapat menyajikan lagu dengan cara yang terasa terkini dan menarik bagi pendengar saat ini.

"Namun, saya melihat tren sebagai referensi, bukan arahan. Tren dapat menginspirasi pilihan produksi tertentu, tetapi tidak pernah mendefinisikan jantung dari sebuah lagu," ujarnya.

Di akhir wawancara, Jeniffer menekankan bahwa kreativitas tidak selalu bisa dipaksakan, dan kembali ke alasan awal membuat musik adalah kunci untuk menemukan inspirasi kembali.

Aurisya Koswara Kwando, Soloist

Melanjutkan rangkaian wawancara spesial Hari Musik Sedunia bersama Kaboom, Aurisya Koswara Kwando membagikan pandangannya mengenai identitas artistik, penceritaan kreatif, dan tanggung jawab emosional sebagai seorang solois di industri musik saat ini. Wawancara ini berlangsung di Aurisya Private Studio, Jakarta Pusat.

Saat wawancara di Aurisya Private Studio, Aurisya menjelaskan bahwa keterlibatannya dalam setiap lagu yang diciptakannya jauh melampaui sekadar rekaman musik. Menurutnya, penulisan lagu, arahan visual, penceritaan, dan konsep pertunjukan semuanya terhubung secara mendalam dan memainkan peran penting dalam membentuk pengalaman emosional di balik karyanya.

"Peran saya dalam setiap lagu yang saya tulis tentu sangat signifikan, dan mempengaruhi setiap aspek yang terlibat dalam prosesnya. Dimulai dengan menulis lirik. Saya selalu mencoba menuangkan tema cinta dan obsesi ke dalam setiap lagu yang saya ciptakan," ujarnya.

Aurisya mengungkapkan bahwa ia menikmati meromantisasi emosi seputar cinta dan kasih sayang, sering mengekspresikan perspektif seorang wanita yang sangat jatuh cinta dan merindukan untuk dicintai sepenuh hati. Baginya, lirik menjadi fondasi yang memungkinkan emosi disampaikan dengan jujur dan lengkap.

Ia juga menekankan bahwa elemen visual seperti konsep karya seni, skema warna, gaya, riasan, ekspresi, dan narasi video musik harus tetap terhubung dengan emosi yang dibawa oleh lagu itu sendiri. Aurisya juga berbicara secara terbuka tentang tekanan emosional yang sering datang saat tampil sendirian di atas panggung.

"Jika seseorang pergi setelah pertunjukan saya merasa ditemani, dipahami, atau bahkan teringat seseorang yang mereka cintai karena lagu yang saya nyanyikan, maka saya merasa telah berhasil memenuhi peran saya sebagai musisi," pungkasnya.

Celeste Vivienne, Soloist
<

Celeste Vivienne turut membagikan pandangannya mengenai hubungan antara musisi dan media sosial, serta tantangan yang dihadapi para penampil solo di industri musik saat ini. Sesi wawancara berlangsung di Lunaria Studio Record bersama Kaboom.

Selama wawancara di Lunaria Studio Record, Celeste Vivienne menjelaskan bahwa media sosial telah menciptakan hubungan yang lebih dekat antara artis dan audiens dibandingkan generasi sebelumnya. Menurutnya, perkembangan ini bisa terasa sangat menginspirasi, namun juga membawa tekanan emosional yang dialami banyak artis di balik layar.

"Media sosial adalah paradoks yang indah bagi saya. Di satu sisi, ini sangat menginspirasi karena saya bisa melihat bagaimana musik saya hidup dalam momen sehari-hari orang, bagaimana musik menjadi bagian dari cerita, penyembuhan, dan kebahagiaan mereka," ujar Celeste.

Ia percaya bahwa tingkat kedekatan ini membuat musik terasa lebih manusiawi, langsung, dan bermakna dari sebelumnya. Namun, di balik koneksi itu, Celeste juga mengakui beban emosional yang datang dengan hidup di bawah visibilitas publik yang konstan.

"Saya telah belajar bahwa melindungi sebagian dari diri saya—sesuatu yang pribadi dan tidak tersentuh—sangatlah penting. Karena pada akhirnya, musik membutuhkan kejujuran, dan kejujuran membutuhkan ruang," ujarnya.

Di akhir wawancara, Celeste menekankan bahwa kesuksesan tidak harus diukur dari seberapa baik seorang artis mengikuti setiap tren, melainkan dari kemampuan menciptakan sesuatu yang abadi dan tetap terasa benar bahkan bertahun-tahun kemudian.

Our Dream, Boy Group

Leonardo Nicholas Wang atau yang akrab dikenal sebagai Leo dari Our Dream turut membagikan pandangannya mengenai kerja sama tim, chemistry grup, dan tantangan mempertahankan identitas musik yang kuat di tengah industri yang terus berubah dengan cepat. Sesi wawancara berlangsung di Kadio Space, kantor pusat The Kachoo, Jakarta Selatan bersama Kaboom.

Menurut Leo, sinkronisasi, chemistry, dan kepercayaan antar anggota adalah elemen penting dalam memberikan penampilan grup yang kuat di atas panggung. Ia menjelaskan bahwa di balik setiap pertunjukan, ada upaya berkelanjutan untuk menjaga kedekatan emosional dan komunikasi yang sehat di dalam tim.

"Benar sekali. Penampilan grup memang membutuhkan sinkronisasi, chemistry, dan kepercayaan yang kuat antar anggota untuk memberikan penampilan terbaik. Kami tidak ingin mengecewakan penggemar yang datang mendukung kami, bukan?" kata Leo sambil tertawa.

Untuk menjaga hubungan yang kuat di belakang layar, Leo berbagi bahwa para anggota biasanya membangun koneksi melalui momen-momen sederhana bersama, seperti makan bersama atau bermain-main di ruang latihan saat istirahat. Ia juga menyebutkan bahwa pemimpin grup dan tim manajemen memainkan peran penting dalam membantu anggota membangun chemistry secara alami.

Meskipun memiliki ikatan yang kuat di dalam grup, Leo mengakui bahwa perbedaan pendapat dan tekanan tidak dapat dihindari, terutama saat mempersiapkan proyek besar seperti comeback. Namun, daripada membiarkan konflik berkembang menjadi masalah yang lebih besar, para anggota memilih menyelesaikannya melalui diskusi dan pengambilan keputusan bersama.

"Our Dream berarti 'mimpi kami', jadi kami ingin terus melindungi mimpi kami dan mimpi orang lain juga, agar mereka bisa terus tumbuh dan akhirnya menjadi kenyataan sambil membawa hal-hal positif bagi orang-orang di sekitar kami," pungkasnya.

Hari Musik Sedunia mengingatkan kita bahwa musik adalah warisan bersama umat manusia. Dari alunan tradisional hingga eksperimen digital, setiap nada membawa cerita dan harapan. Mari terus dukung para musisi, apresiasi karya mereka, dan biarkan musik menjadi jembatan yang menyatukan perbedaan. Karena pada akhirnya, musik bukan hanya tentang suara, tetapi tentang rasa yang menyentuh hati setiap pendengarnya.
